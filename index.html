Brandon Gilliam-Head Coach

Brandon Gilliam has been a part of the Men's Soccer Program for 15 years. Brandon joined the program in 2002 as a freshman. He was a starter for 4 years and was #1 in the nation for PDL as a Goalkeeper his final year. Brandon quickly transitioned from a player to a coach in 2008. As an assistant Brandon had a hand in every aspect of the program from player development to player selection, to merchandise, scheduling, and so much more.

email-gilliam@byu.edu

Steve Magleby-Assistant Coach

Coach Magleby starts his third season with BYU in 2017. Steve played for the BYU Men's team in 2002 as a freshman and, after serving a mission to Bolivia, rejoined the team in 2005-2009. Steve grew up in Gaithersburg, Maryland and enjoyed a successful high school and club career in the Washington DC Metro area. While at BYU, Steve served as team captain for his final two seasons with the team. In 2007 Coach Magleby was invited to join the Real Salt Lake preseason training camp. Throughout the 2007 season he spent time with the Real Salt Lake Reserves while continuing to play for BYU as well. After graduating in 2009 he went on to play professionally for Skjold Boldklubben of the Danish 2nd Division. Coach Magleby brings years of experience coaching at the youth levels and has also spent time as a coaching assistant with professional club Barnachea FC of the Chilean Primera B division. He will also serve as the team's Recruiting Coordinator.
